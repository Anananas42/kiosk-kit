# KioskKit Landing Page — Design Brief

## The Product

KioskKit replaces the whiteboard in your common area. It's a touchscreen tablet — guests tap what they took, you see what they owe. That's it.

This is a **hardware product**. The landing page should treat it as such — not a SaaS marketing page, but a product listing. Like stumbling onto a product on Amazon or Alza. Familiar, shoppable, immediately clear that this is something you can buy.

## The Design

Quietly impressive craft. The polish of Stripe or Linear, but applied to a product page layout that feels like an e-shop. The engineering confidence comes through in density and attention to detail, not in flashy animations or SaaS tropes.

### Color & Theme

- **Primarily dark** — deep navy or slate as the dominant surface. Not pure black.
- **Interlaced with lighter sections** for rhythm and contrast.
- One confident accent color for CTAs and interactive elements.
- The product image area can have a cleaner, well-lit feel to make the hardware stand out.

### Typography

- **Clean sans-serif throughout** (Inter or similar). No serif, no italic.
- Tight letter-spacing on headlines.
- Dense and informative — every element earns its place.

### Shape & Detail

- Medium border radius (~6-8px). Not bubbly/round, not sharp/square.
- Subtle animation: hero fade-in + slight upward translate on load. Nothing else.

### What It's NOT

- Not a SaaS template or corporate marketing page.
- Not cold, techy, or developer-oriented.
- Not playful or whimsical.
- Not spread out with big gaps and thin content.

## Audience

Non-technical pension owners, small hotel operators, apartment hosts. People who run a 10-room guesthouse in the countryside. The page should feel inviting and familiar — like shopping for a product, not evaluating enterprise software.

## Reference Vibes

The familiarity of an Amazon/Alza product listing, combined with the craft and density of Stripe or Linear. Custom enough to signal quality, conventional enough that the user immediately knows what to do: look at the product, read the price, add to cart.
