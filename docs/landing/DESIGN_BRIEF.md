# KioskKit Landing Page — Design Brief

## The Product

KioskKit replaces the whiteboard next to your guest fridge. It's a touchscreen tablet — guests tap what they took, you see what they owe. That's it.

The product is boring on purpose. No mystery, no hype. The customer should instantly understand what it does, what it costs, and whether it's for them.

## The Design

The design should be quietly impressive. Like how Apple presents AirTag — mundane product, immaculate presentation. The craft signals "this was made by someone who really knows what they're doing." Swiss army knife energy — trustworthy, capable, no-nonsense.

### Color & Theme

- **Primarily dark** — deep navy or dark slate as the dominant surface. Not pure black.
- **Interlaced with lighter sections** for rhythm and contrast. The alternation creates visual interest.
- Not pure dark mode, not light mode — easy on the eyes.
- One confident accent color for CTAs. Not flashy, not muted.

### Shape & Detail

- Medium border radius (~6–8px). Not bubbly/round, not sharp/square.
- Dense and informative — every element earns its place.
- Subtle animation: hero fade-in + slight upward translate on load. Nothing else.

### What It's NOT

- Not a SaaS template or corporate marketing page.
- Not cold, techy, or developer-oriented.
- Not playful or whimsical.
- Not spread out with big gaps and thin content.
- Not "boring design for a boring product" — the product is boring, the design is polished.

## Layout

- **Hero is the main event.** Full-width, immersive, takes up the viewport. Dark background. Large serif headline, human subtitle, kiosk UI mockup showing the actual product interface.
- **Below the hero is compact and scannable.** Alternating dark/light sections.
  - Footer — minimal

## Audience

Non-technical pension owners, small hotel operators, apartment hosts. People who run a 10-room guesthouse in the countryside. The page should feel inviting, not intimidating. The engineering confidence comes through in craft, not in jargon.

## Reference Vibes

The polish of Stripe, Linear, or Vercel — but the message is simple and human. Think lean YC startup pages — clearly custom, really appealing, not template-y. Dense like a good tool, not sparse like a billboard.
